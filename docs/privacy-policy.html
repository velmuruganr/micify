Micify

Privacy Policy · Last updated: June 2026
🔒 Micify collects no personal data, requires no account, and makes no network requests.

What data does Micify collect?

None. Micify does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal information, usage data, or audio recordings.

Microphone access

Micify requests microphone permission solely to relay live audio to your selected output device (Bluetooth speaker, wired headset, or earpiece). Audio is processed entirely on-device in real time and is never recorded, saved, or transmitted anywhere.

Bluetooth access

Bluetooth permission is requested to detect connected audio output devices and to enable automatic relay start when a Bluetooth speaker connects. No Bluetooth device identifiers are stored or shared.

Phone state access

Micify requests phone state permission to automatically pause the relay during incoming calls and resume it afterwards. No call details, numbers, or identities are accessed or stored.

Analytics and advertising

The free version of Micify may display ads served by Google AdMob. AdMob may collect device identifiers and usage data in accordance with Google's Privacy Policy. No personal data from Micify is shared with AdMob beyond what the AdMob SDK collects directly.

Paid users see no ads. No analytics SDKs are included in any version of Micify.

Children's privacy

Kids Mode in Micify is designed for use by children under the supervision of a parent or teacher. No data is collected from child users. COPPA compliance flags are set for Kids Mode screens when ads are present.

Data stored on device

Micify stores only the following locally on your device, using Android SharedPreferences:

  • Whether the first-launch tip has been shown (a single boolean flag)

This data never leaves your device.

Third-party services

Micify uses no third-party SDKs except Google AdMob (free tier only). No crash reporting, analytics, or remote configuration services are used.

Open source

Micify is open source under the MIT Licence. The full source code is available at github.com/velmuruganr/micify. You can verify these privacy claims by reading the code directly.
